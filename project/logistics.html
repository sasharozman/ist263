Where Can I Park My Tiny House?

Needless to say, this is the biggest problem that comes with tiny houses. Zoning rules and laws vary from state to state, county to county, and even town to town.
So, where can you park your home? Here are the best places:

RV Parks and Campgrounds

This may not sound ideal at first, but give it a chance. Often private RV parks create special spaces for tiny houses, thus grouping together and creating a mini-community.
A private RV park has fewer regulations than a public one which can limit how long you are able to stay. Campgrounds often have the ability for a tiny house to hook up to
public supplies.

Federal Land

There are over 600 million acres across America owned and overseen by the federal government. Federal land is opened to “dispersed camping” meaning you are able to stay
and park on the land for free. This is the best option if you want to travel with your tiny home.

Tiny House Communities

From California to Texas, tiny house communities are growing exponentially. This allows for an amazing community feel with shared interests. Many communities offer power,
water, and internet hookups for a monthly cost. Very often, they will have events and amenities from BBQs, walking trails, and even pools.

Private-Owned Land

Thousands of tiny house owners are connecting with people looking to make some extra cash through the Facebook group “Tiny House Hosting”. With over 18,000 members this
Facebook group helps “connect land-owners with tiny house people and bridge the connection.” Whether that’s farmland or a piece of land right outside the city, this group may be
the best option while saving money for land of your own.

Your Own Land

While this may be the most expensive option upfront, this is ideal for the long term. With owning your own land you decide what you can and want to do with it such as parking your
own tiny home. This option is the most private and offers a greater sense of security and stability.
